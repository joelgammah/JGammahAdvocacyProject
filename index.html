About My Topic

Racial injustice refers to the unequal treatment and discrimination of individuals based on their race. In the United States, racial injustice has a long history, dating back to the country's founding and the institution of slavery. Even after the abolition of slavery, racial discrimination and segregation persisted, and continue to be a significant problem in the United States today.

Impact!

In the United States, racial minorities generally refer to people who are not white. This includes people who identify as Black, Indigenous, Latinx, Asian, and other non-white racial groups. While the term "minority" can be used to describe any group that is not in the majority, it is often used to refer to racial and ethnic groups that have historically been disadvantaged and still facing racial injustice.
